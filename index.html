Project Title: Moodle (Redesign) An online Learning Management System
Project Duration: July '13 - Nov '13

We started with the problem observation; difficulty in sharing notes, difficulty in submitting assignments, and difficulty in having a course related discussion.

The college provides MOODLE, a Learning Management System, but still students and faculty do not use this. They are using different platforms for sharing notes, discussion and project submission.

Then, we performed a Heuristic Evaluation of Moodle and found out the usability bugs in the interface. After competitive Market analysis and user research, we set up a benchmark for our design.
We created workflows, system interactions and feature followed by an in depth design survey which directed users to select grids and colour palettes of their own preference on the basis of which the LMS would be developed.

Project Title: Me.Nion: Exploring tangible interactions for capturing self photography
Project Guide: : Prof. Keyur Sorathia
Project Duration: Jan '14 - Apr '14
Academic Paper: click me

Self-Photography better known as 'selfies' have become a trend due to incoming of Social Networks. Selfies are cumbersome task to handle because of difficulty in setting the correct frame and triggering the capture. In our research work we found that, not much have changed in terms of interaction while taking photograph. Hence, we explored different action and the tangible interactions to trigger the actions for capturing the photographs. We also explored different scenarios for selfies. We argue that the tangible interactions which we identified not only provided the better way for selfies but also provided playful actions for taking photograph.

A me.nion camera sees information about the surrounding and captures the picture in real time. The prototype is divided into two parts- The upper part contains the camera which is triggered by the lower body whenever the user wants to capture the selfie.

The user interacts with the lower part through a button press to click a self-photograph which can be seen through the digital display device enabled in the prototype. The zoom in - zoom out functionality can be enabled through rotating the hands of the lower body which functions through an inbuilt potentiometer.
